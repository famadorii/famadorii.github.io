Landscapes of Longing

dissertation

My dissertation, "Landscapes of Longing: Migration and the Remaking of Rural Mexico," is a sweeping history of how Mexican emigrants saved rural Mexico. I explore why emigrants left their homes and how they remained attached to their patria chicas or little fatherlands. Focusing on towns from Los Altos de Jalisco during the second half of the twentith century, I explore how emigrants acted as distance residents through remittances, cultural productions, and activism while also shaping their new residencies.

The Preservers and Retrievers of Stories

archival creation

During my fieldwork in rural Mexico, I came across a unorganized documents in a small town dating back to the 1800s. I decided to organize and establish a historical in the town. After six weeks of a dozen volunteers going through close to 15,000 documents, the archive was set. Read more about my experience in my AHA columns.
